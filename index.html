I'm currently a first-year Ph.D. student at Northwestern University, advised by Jason Hartline. My research interests lie at the intersection of theoretical computer science and economics. While I have a broad interest in economics and computation, I'm especially interested in market design for school choice, mechanism design for education, behavioral economics, and learning theory. I like to think about how tools from theoretical computer science, AI, and economic design can help solve problems of social import.

Keywords: Algorithmic game theory, mechanism design, behavioral economics.
